What is an Arctic fox?

The Arctic fox looks similar to the common red fox but smaller, furrier, and pure white in color. Its compact body features a number of adaptations that allow it to live in the icy Arctic regions.

Where do Arctic foxes live?

The Arctic fox habitat range, when viewed on a map, is a consistent line covering all the northern coasts on the planet. The specific countries and islands where the Arctic foxes can be found include the northernmost areas of Canada, Finland, Greenland, Iceland, Norway, Russia, Svalbard, Sweden, and the US (Alaska).

What is the Arctic fox's scientific name?

The Arctic fox;s scientific name is Vulpes lagopus. The species grows dense fur on its feet during the winter, earning it the name lagopus, which means rabbit-footed.
